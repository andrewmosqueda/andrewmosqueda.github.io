Three level AC Inverter Simulation

The circuit below is a PID controlled 40KHz PWM based converter that generates a 60Hz 120Vrms waveform from two 225Vdc supplies (V1 and V9 in the circuit below). 
M1 MOSFET is the switch used to increase the output magnitude for the positive cycle. When output magnitude is increased during the positive cycle, the output capacitor C1 charges from the positive DC bus (V1).
M2 MOSFET is the switch used to increase the output magnitude for the negative cycle. When output magnitude is increased during the negative cycle, the output capacitor C1 charges from the negative DC bus (V9).
M3 and M4 MOSFETs are simultaneous and are used to continue the current on L1 after M1/M2. M1 and M4 are also used to decrease the magnitude on the output. When output magnitude is decreased, the output capacitor C1 discharges to the positive DC bus (V1) during the positive cycle and to the negative DC bus (V9) during the negative cycle.
L2, L3, L4 and L5 are just parasitic inductances. Its values can be changed to see the effect on the MOSFET voltage spikes or output ripple.
C6, R10, C9, R11, C7, C8, C2 and R2 are dampers to lessen the voltage spikes across the MOSFETs.
B3, R3, B4, R4, B5, R5, B6 and R6 are simplified gate drive circuits. In real application, gate drive circuits have its own isolated supply and usually optically controlled.
R1 is the load. In this application, full load is 42 ohms.

Below is the truth table for the switches M1, M2, M3 and M4.

Controller

VREF (Input) – Reference signal to be generated by the inverter.
VFEED (Input) – Actual voltage output of the inverter. This input can have ripple so an anti-alias filter is needed. The anti-alias filter should filter out bandwidth greater than 20KHz (Half the bandwidth of the switching frequency).
CFEED (Input) – Actual current on L1 inductor filter. Filtering is not the same with VFEED because the actual switching current need to be detected fast.
SAW (Input) – Triangle signal to be used for the PWM control.
PG (Input) – Proportional gain control constant.
IG (Input) – Integral gain control constant.
DG (Input) – Differential gain control constant.
S1 (Output) – controls the switching of M1 MOSFET.
+SW (Output) – controls the switching of M3 MOSFET. Signal is equal to -SW.
S3 (Output) – controls the switching of M2 MOSFET.
-SW (Output) – controls the switching of M4 MOSFET. Signal is equal to +SW.
PID_OUT (Output) – PID value debug output.
PID_SH_CLK (Output) – Miscellaneous debug output.
