PID_CONTROLLER

What's inside the PID_CONTROLLER?

Below is the schematic of the PID Controller I designed for LTSPICE simulation of the Buck Circuit. The "error" signal which is the difference of the TARGET and FEEDBACK is fed into a sample and hold circuit. The HOLD_OUT is the error data sampled at a rate of 40KHz ( the circuit samples every rise and fall of the CLK input). The reason for holding the error data is to have a fix error and therefore fix duty in every clock cycle of the switch mode system. The HOLD_OUT output is then amplified in the POUT section, integrated in the IOUT section and differentiated in the DOUT section. The sum of the PID is then compared to a 40KHz triangle signal to generate the PWM control signal (SW_OUT).

Sample and Hold

The error signal which is the difference of the TARGET and FEEDBACK  is sampled (HOLD_OUT) during the rise and fall of the 20KHz CLK signal. Therefore the sampling frequency is 2 x 20KHz = 40KHz.

INTEGRATOR

The output of the sample and hold block (HOLD_OUT) is multiplied by the integrator gain IG before it is fed to the integrator block. The integrator block adds the input to the IOUT value every rise and fall of the 20KHz CLK signal. Therefore the operating frequency is 2 x 20KHz = 40KHz.

See Inside

DIFFERENTIATOR

The output of the sample and hold block (HOLD_OUT) is multiplied by the differentiator gain DG before it is fed to the differentiator block. The differentiator block holds the difference of the current error value to the previous error value every rise and fall of the 20KHz CLK signal. Therefore the operating frequency is 2 x 20KHz = 40KHz. This block also reuses the Sample and Hold block to hold the current and previous values of the error.
