Paniman Beach, Puerto Azul, Cavite

Just the right destination for a day tour adventurer

Camera: Samsung Galaxy A3 2017, Iphone 6

This is my favorite beach in Cavite. I think the water is clearer than the waters of Calayo, Nasugbu.  But beware there is a river on the south side of the cove so the water may get murky when there is  heavy rain. The entrance fee is 300 PHP per person. The shaded tables are free to occupy on a first come first serve basis. No reservation. 

Globe Telecom signal is clear and internet surfing is well perhaps because we are the only users on that place.

Paniman beach is just one of the place on Puerto Azul. On the main entrance of Puerto Azul, tell the guard that you are going to Paniman so he will let you enter.

MY ADVICE

Bring your own water bucket, paper and some toilet air freshener. There is no water on the toilet so imagine what happens when you poop. There is one shower on the beach.

They are strict on the 5 PM leaving time. The beach watcher told me it is because they do not have electricity around the place. So this beach is strictly for day tour only.

I think the nearest store is 5KM uphill away. Bring your own food, water and cooking materials. There are places where you can grill. 

Mosquito. That was the word that my elementary classmates used to tease me. It reminds me my family name (Mosqueda) everytime I stumble a place with lot of mosquitoes. So first of all, this place has lots of mosquitoes perhaps because of the swamp, river and the forest behind this beautiful place. So my advice, apply mosquito lotion, put an organic Citronella refreshener to your car to repel them from entering your car and close the doors immediately. Going home from the beach, I kept on stopping the car just to let out those pesky mosquitoes.  

Park away from the coconut trees.
